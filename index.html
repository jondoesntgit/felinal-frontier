The Felinal Frontier

Note: this game was designed to be a low-tech game that can be played on a sheet of paper with a simple random number generator. You can use a stopwatch in real life with the lap functionality. If a stopwatch is not available, one can use dice, coins, etc... For a stopwatch, find the hundredths on a split, and 0/1 is computer damage, 2/3 is reactor, etc... The damage can be found by the number of tenths away from your desired boost time. Make the game your own. It was designed to be a way to kill time with friends. Extend it as you will.

You command a crew of kittens aboard the USS Catnip. You are hauling 10 tonnes of catnip. Unforutnately, you have fallen into a hostile swarm of space rats that want to see your demise. Your goal is to survive as long as possible. (Note: in the real life version, if you're in a car or at a restaurant, you are awaiting rescue in the form of arriving at your destination or getting your food.)

There are 5 key systems on your ship: computers banks, nuclear reactor, engines, space rat evasion system, and the power cable network. Your ship needs to boost periodically. On pen and paper, this should be once per 60 seconds. Everytime your ship boosts, there is a possibility that one of the key systems might break, and will need repair.

Each system can be repaired by a kitten. Kittens can repair a default of 2 damage, but specialists in an area can repair 3. It takes a full boost period for a kitten to arrive at a location on the ship, so choose wisely.

If a system becomes too damaged, systems the rely on it will also become damaged. For example, if the reactor breaks down (default 4+ damage), then the engines will also begin to break down. If the rat evasion system breaks, then rats will get into power cables and chew them apart.

Additionally, if a system suffers significant damage (default 8+ damage), then it will ruin some of your catnip. If all of your catnip gets ruined, you lose.
